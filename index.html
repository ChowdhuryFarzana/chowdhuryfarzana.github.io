About Me

I am a senior at Mount Holyoke College double majoring in Computer Science and Statistics. I am applying to PhD programs for Fall 2026, driven by a passion for developing AI systems that address meaningful real-world challenges.

My research interests center on machine learning for education, natural language processing, computational biology, and robotics. I am particularly drawn to work that bridges multiple disciplines, whether that's using NLP to understand student learning patterns, applying computational methods to accelerate drug discovery, or developing intelligent systems that can reason and interact with complex environments. I believe the most impactful advances in AI come from combining rigorous technical work with deep attention to societal applications.

Beyond academics, I serve as President of the Bangladeshi Student Association and work as a Resident Advisor, roles that have strengthened my commitment to building communities.

Research Interests

  • Machine Learning & Education : Developed a MCMC-inspired pipelines with large language models for interpretable student persona generation and performance prediction.
  • Computational Biology & Drug Discovery: Investigating protein-ligand binding affinity through Probabilistic Roadmap motion planning algorithms with energy-based evaluation. Benchmarking computational methods against industry standards to accelerate drug discovery processes.
  • Robotics & Motion Planning: Exploring multi-robot coordination, autonomous systems optimization, and path planning algorithms. Integrating classical motion planning techniques with modern computational approaches for improved efficiency and accuracy.
  • Natural Language Processing: Large language model applications, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems, and AI-powered information systems for real-world applications.
